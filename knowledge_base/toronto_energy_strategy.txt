Toronto is the primary energy and infrastructure hub for our telecom network across Canada.
Due to the density of telecom sites and the concentration of high-traffic network demand,
Toronto is considered the most critical region for energy optimization.

Key priorities for the Toronto region include:
- Improving grid resilience and backup power reliability.
- Investing in battery storage and smart microgrid technologies.
- Reducing peak power consumption during high-load periods.
- Implementing AI-driven predictive energy management for large urban sites.

Toronto’s telecom infrastructure consumes significantly more power than any other Canadian
region due to population density and enterprise traffic. Therefore, it is essential that
all energy efficiency programs and sustainability initiatives prioritize Toronto first.

Future planning should focus on:
- Renewable energy procurement in the Greater Toronto Area.
- Advanced cooling technologies for urban data centers.
- Infrastructure upgrades to support electric grid transitions.
